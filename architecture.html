My Architecture Work

While I have been interested in pursuing architecture since I was little, my technical journey did not start until high school. In high school, I learned the basics of creating buildings with AutoCAD and Revit. I built on this foundation in college, where I primarily used Rhinocerous 3D as well as InDesign and Photoshop.

The House and The View

Rhino, 2022

In 1998, photographer Richard Misrach took a series of photos along "Cancer Alley", a part of the Mississippi River with a heavy and detrimental industrial presence. One of his photos, "Cypress Swamp, Alligator Bayou, Prairieville, Louisiana" (Slide 8), served as an inspiration for an artist’s studio that focused on the view. The studio, inspired by Studio Gang's Arcus Center for Social Justice, Tod Williams+Billie Tsein's The Barnes Foundation, and Robert Venturi's Brant-Johnson Ski House, features an interior facade made of tree trunks. It also contains an office area with a large viewing window, a dock accessible by boat, storage, and hanging fixtures to display art.

The Vertical Home

Rhino, 2023

The Vertical Home is a design influenced by a group of clients with varying needs: five college students and their dog. The limited footprint of the project was the primary constraint, but the home was able to have as many floors as needed. The first floor, which is the most accessible for all occupants, contains a kitchen and areas for dining, living, and laundry. The three floors below and the two above have a bathroom, desk, and a bedroom for each student that can be closed off to the communal stairs. The roof serves as an additional lounge space with a small garden.

The Sectional Home

Rhino, 2022

The Sectional Home, while having the same clients as The Vertical Home, provided a new challenge of each room needing to be unique in section. I took inspiration from the shape of a cartoon lightning bolt as well as SANAA's New Museum to create "A Shocking Construction". Each floor is accessible by a set of stairs as well as an elevator. Each floor also contains a balcony with each balcony serving a different purpose, such as a flower garden, a vegetable garden, a shaded lounge area, and a dog-centered area.

Jefferson Island Museum

Rhino, Museum Board, Card Stock, 2022

The abnormal height of Jefferson Island, caused by a salt dome, is what gives this location its name. This salt dome, originally home to extensive salt mines, experienced an oil rig accident that filled the mines with water. There were no casualties, but the mines were closed off afterwards. Using the abandoned mines as inspiration, I designed a visitor center/museum for people to learn about the history of the site. The influence for the center came from the shapes and angles of the mines, Eduardo Souto de Moura's Uitzicht Crematorium, and Thomas Phifer's Glenstone Museum. The building is partially embedded in the ground, with sloped earth leading down to the front entrance. This allows for a public observation area on one part of the building and a private office space on the other.

The Tulane Depot

Rhino, 2022

Tulane University is home to thousands of archival materials branching across multiple areas of study. These materials are rarely viewed, however, because there is not enough space to safely display them. The Tulane Depot is a museum complete with office spaces, study spaces, and room for different types of displays. The pattern of the walls and shelves, inspired by John Hejduk’s Diamond Museum C (Slide 2), create different spatial relationships, such as private areas for study and public areas for collaboration.

The Golden Eagle Luxury Train

Rhino, 2023

The Golden Eagle Luxury Train plan was created from a study about inhabiting small spaces as a precursor to my Vertical and Sectional Home projects. This study, complete with scaled figures, allowed me to play with the idea of just how little space was needed for a person to live comfortably. Even with a small footprint, these rooms contain large beds, lounging areas, and a complete bathroom while still leaving space in the carriage for mechanical and storage rooms.

The Public Porch and the Private Backyard

Rhino, Photoshop, 2024

This project was designed with the unhoused community of New Orleans in mind. Most of the housing lots in New Orleans are very long with little width, with a major challenge being creating a community without everyone being able to reach the street. This design emphasizes the idea of celebrating the common front porch culture of the south, while also providing a private back yard for security. This plan is made up of two duplexes which share a backyard wall to maximize the square footage and provide a variety of floor plan options. The windows are optimized for community viewing and letting in eastern and northern light to avoid the heat of southern exposure.

The Gardening Community

Rhino, Chipboard with Screen Mesh, 2024

The Gardening Community evolved from plans for URBANbuild 20, a Tulane student-run design-build program, for Miro Street in the 9th ward. The program I participated in was for designing tiny homes for the unhouses population in New Orleans in collaboration with Louvis Services. My design expanded on the idea of giving the rehabilitated a community-helping job, such as growing food in a garden. It also supports the front porch culture of the south, with a public front porch and lounging area as well as a screened-in side porch for privacy and protection from bugs and the elements. The interior features a movable wall, allowing for different programs in different rooms, depending on the user’s needs.

USS Cod Submarine Memorial for NAWIC

AutoCAD, 2020

I designed this museum for a competition with National Association of Women in Construction (NAWIC) in high school. This project taught me the fundamentals of architectural drafting, such as how to create schedules, design electrical plans, and properly dimension plans. As a public museum with private space, the plan features a private hallway as well as a private second floor for fragile artifacts. We also had a limited square footage to work with, so the necessary amenities needed to be included while leaving space for an assembly area. The roof and exterior facade are made up of metal flashing, inspired by the nearby public buildings to the submarine.
